West Brom mascot Richard Eades dies before Boxing Day game against Everton

blog

WEST BROM fans, staff and former players have paid tribute after their club mascot tragically died ahead of the match against Everton.

Richard Eades, who was a father of two aged 42, passed away before the Boxing Day fixture at The Hawthorns.

Eades has dressed up as Albi the mascot for 14 years and ran the West Brom supporters' club Boing FC.

A Baggies statement said: "Albion are mourning the loss of long-serving employee Richard Eades, who has passed away at the age of just 42.

"Richard, pictured left, embarked on almost 30 years’ service at the Club as a 14-year-old - working as a member of the ground staff and then the coaching staff before taking on the roles of several Albion mascots, including 'Baggie Bird', 'Baggie Junior', and, from 2007, ‘Albi’.

"He also played, coached and was manager of ‘Boing FC’ - Albion’s official supporters’ football team. Rich was a highly-respected Birmingham County FA referee.

"He amassed many friends at The Hawthorns, all of whom were shocked and deeply saddened by his untimely death.

"The thoughts of all at West Bromwich Albion are with his fiancé Carly and his two young children, Lily-Mae, 8, and Archie, 3.

"The Club will pay a full tribute to the much-loved father of two and Albion devotee before the New Year’s Eve fixture against Arsenal."

Mascots Baggie Bird and Albi dance off ahead of West Brom match at the Hawthorns in 2013

News of his death broke before the match against Everton on Tuesday, the Birmingham Mail report.

Tributes have poured in on social media for Eades.

Former Albion defender Paul Robinson tweeted: "So sad to just hear the news that Richard Eades has sadly passed away. My thoughts and prayers go out to his family at this difficult time."

His match-day partner Dave Challoner, who dresses up as Baggie Bird, posted a picture of the pair together and said: "Sorry guys it true. Thanks for all the messages sleep easy @Richard_Eades."
