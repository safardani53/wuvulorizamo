Netflix fans convinced late rapper Lil Peep was MURDERED after controversial Everybodys Everythin

July 13, 2024
blog

A NETFLIX documentary about the late rapper Lil Peep has raised questions about his death, with fans convinced he was murdered.

The rapper was found dead on his tour bus at the age of just 21, with the cause of death ruled as an accidental overdose.

But the new documentary, Everybody's Everything has got people talking, with many becoming suspicious about exactly how the rapper died.

Lil Peep, whose real name was Gustav Ahr, was found dead on his tour bus in Arizona in 2017.

The star tested positive for Fentanyl, Xanax, marijuana, cocaine and Tramadol, as well as a number of other powerful prescription opiates.

The documentary explored whether Lil Peep died by accident, or if he took his own life.

His brother Gustav was adamant it was an accidental overdose, describing his brother as "super happy with where he was in life" and insisting he "was not struggling".

Private messages between Lil Peep and 20-year-old fan Mariah Bons surfaced soon after his death.

In the messages Bons described getting the star "high af" and that she "tried to get him to say hi to my brother but he wouldn’t wake up."

The documentary revealed the Xanax Bons may have given Lil Peep was laced with Fentanyl and he did not know it.

An ex of Mariah's described her as "shady" and claimed she had previously sold them "bad Xanax" which they said "almost killed them".

Police initially investigated Mariah after Lil Peep's death, but subsequently cleared her of any wrongdoing.

Mariah denied any responsibility in his death and she also died at the age of 21 in July last year.

Fans watching the documentary became convinced Lil Peep was murdered and took to Twitter in anger.

"Lil Peep was murdered and no one can change my mind," wrote one person on Twitter.

Another added: "Someone killed Lil Peep intentionally, I’m convinced."

"LIL PEEP WAS MURDERED," exclaimed one Twitter user, while another went deeper on the conspiracy theory.

"That Lil Peep documentary was crazy... I really believe they killed that n***a cuz he was about to cut them all off," they wrote.
