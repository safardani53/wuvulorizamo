Demi Lovato has been through A LOT, folks.

As the singer herself recently chronicled upon celebrating her five years of sobriety, Lovato has suffered through an eating disorder, hardcore drug use and a past that includes self-mutilation.

So if you think she’d be bothered by the release of a semi-naked photo against her will, well …

… you’d be very wrong.

Indeed, Lovato has been included in the latest round of illegal hacking by some sick individual who broke into the accounts of such stars as Emma Watson and Amanda Seyfried.

This total and complete loser has stolen naked pictures of these female stars and shared the images on 4Chan.

According to that website, the pictures range from "tame selfies to explicitly sexual."

The hack job is reminiscent of 2014’s The Fappening.

During that event, Jennifer Lawrence, Kate Upton and many others also had their nude bodies exposed to the world by an unethical computer nerd.

In this latest case, insiders have hinted that Watson and Seyfried may pursue legal recourse against the hacker.

But it doesn’t sound as if Lovato will follow suit.

"I love how everyone’s freaking out about one picture. It’s not nude and it’s just cleavage," Lovato Tweeted in response to the scandal.

We’re not going to post the picture here because we have morals.

"Besides the world has seen me nude BY CHOICE before.. #vanityfair #nicetry #cleavagegameonpoint #myboobsarenteventhatbig #angles."

It’s true.

Lovato received some flak for that magazine spread, but explained at the time why she chose to pose naked.

"I want to inspire other women," Demi said in October of 2015, adding:

"For someone who’s had body image issues since they were a child, I went from hating every inch of my body to showing every inch of my body to the entire world."

"And without touching up anything," she adds.

"I wouldn’t have done that six months ago, because I didn’t have the confidence that I do today."
