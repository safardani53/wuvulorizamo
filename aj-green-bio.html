Early Life & Education

Early Life

AJ Green was born Adriel Jeremiah Green on July 31, 1988, in Summerville, South Carolina, United States. His father, Woodrow Green, was a steelworker and truck driver and his mother, Dora Green, was a clerk at a Walmart.

He lost his only sibling, an older brother, Avionce Green, in a car accident on the way to a school carnival.

AJ was just 4 at the time of the incident, the same incident left their aunt paralyzed too.

Interestingly, the former NFL player was on a juggling team in elementary school. He learned to juggle in second grade and could juggle as many as four items at once.

Education

AJ Green attended Summerville High School in South Carolina, where he played basketball and track in addition to football.

He played as a wide receiver at Summerville under the head coach John McKissick. He showcased amazing football in high school and was considered one of the top football prospects of the Class of 2008.

Similarly, he was ranked No. 9 on the Rivals.com list for the high school class of 2008.

After a successful high school career, Green made his commitment to Georgia by signing a letter of intent to play for the Georgia Bulldogs football team.

He played under the head coach Mark Richt at Georgia from 2008 to 2010 and declared for the draft.

Career

College football career went excellently well for AJ, which helped him get picked fourth overall in the 2011 NFL Draft by the Cincinnati Bengals.

He broke the mark set by Lindsay Scott and became the highest-drafted wide receiver in Georgia school history.

On July 2011, he signed a four-year rookie contract with the Bengals.

His gameplay as a rookie earned him a Pro Bowl nomination in addition to being named to the NFL All-Rookie Team.

Similarly, Green was ranked 77th by his peers on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2012.

And after 10 amazing seasons with the Bengals, he was traded to the Cardinals on a year long deal worth $6 million on March 17, 2021.

The following year on April 14, 2022, Green signed a one-year contract extension with the Cardinals worth $3.5 million.

A.J. Green announced his retirement on February 6, 2023, capping off a stellar career. In his 12 seasons long NFL career, he recorded 10,514 yards, 70 touchdowns in receiving, 93 yards in rushing, and 12 fumbles in regular seasons.
