DWTS fans slam Tyra Banks' over the top &amp; unflattering outfit on Elvis night after she fumbles

April 18, 2024

DANCING with the Stars fans have slammed Tyra Banks’s “over the top” and “unflattering” outfit for Elvis night.

It comes after the host was criticised for making live TV blunders during the premiere episode last week.

For week two of the competition, DWTS celebrated the music of Elvis Presley, and host Tyra Banks, 48, chose an outfit to match.

She wore a revealing satin red dress, which she paired with knee-length leather boots and a tight leather blazer, accessorising with a huge oversized belt.

Tyra’s hair was styled with a 60s blowout to match the retro theme.

Meanwhile, her co-host Alfonso Ribeiro, 51, looked dapper in a navy blue two-piece, which he paired with a muted shirt and tie.

But not many viewers were falling in love with Tyra’s Elvis-inspired look, and took to Twitter to share their opinions.

“That outfit is not flattering on Tyra,” posted one fan on Twitter.

“Alfonso kept it simple wearing a suit and tie, while Tyra’s outfit is over the top!” wrote another.

Many others simply asked: “What is Tyra wearing?”

Since taking over hosting duties in 2020, Tyra has been known for her outrageous live TV looks.

The model previously made frequent outfit changes every show.

But as DWTS now streams live on Disney+ with no commercial breaks, Tyra must stick to one choice for the whole two hours.

ON A TY-RADE

Meanwhile, the host got off to a rocky start last week as she made a major on-screen error, and then blamed the show’s producers for the mistake.

Reading out the leaderboard after the first eight couples had performed, she began: "At the top for now, are Daniel [Durant] and Britt [Stewart],” while the board instead showed Wayne Brady and Whitney Carson at number one.

But she quickly backtracked: "Oh, that was wrong. Wrong information, ok."

Tyra held her finger to her earpiece and said: "Ok, you're saying at the top is Wayne.

“Wayne is at the top with... Whitney, right?" she asked, hesitating.

Later on in the show, it was time to review the scores again.

"After a dozen dances, here's a look at tonight's leaderboard, and the team got it right this time," she snarked, in a subtle diss to the show's producers.

"So sorry, Wayne, they didn't get it right the first time."
