20 Unlikely Celebrity Friendships That Will Make You Say, &quot;Huh!&quot;

August 24, 2024
blog

These are the celebrity pairs that you wouldn't expect, like Matthew McConaughey and Guy Fieri, Pitbull and John Travolta, and Ed Sheeran and Courteney Cox.

You know those pictures of the cheetah and the dog that are best friends and hang out all the time even though that kind of interspecies friendship is highly unlikely? Well, this is kind of kind that, only with celebrities. These are the famous people who you never would have guessed are actually super good friends — twosomes (and one foursome) we were delighted to discover enjoy each others' company. Some of these unlikely celebrity friendships are more surprising than others, but if you think about any of them for long enough, they start to make sense.

Martha Stewart and Brendan Fraser

At this point, Martha Stewart and Snoop Dogg's friendship is as American as apple pie. But did you know she also hangs out and goes horseback riding with Brendan Fraser? In fact, she Instagrammed this picture! It kind of makes sense; they both have horses on their properties in Westchester County, NY. But it took me a sec to think about what they talk about on their rides... other than their four-legged rides.

Katy Perry and Allison Williams

Not only are Katy Perry and Allison Williams friends. They're best friends. They met several years ago through a mutual pal and "just clicked," according to the Daily Mail. Ever since then, they've been inseparable. Katy even sang at Allison's wedding to her (now ex) husband Ricky Van Deen. 

Jack McBrayer and Alexander Skarsgård

This might be my favorite pair of the whole bunch. Jack McBrayer, best known for playing Kenneth the Page on 30 Rock, and Alexander Skarsgård, best known for his roles in True Blood and Big Little Lies, are BFFs in real life. They've attended award shows together, spent time out at sea in the Arctic for Greenpeace, and posted about each other on Instagram. The friendship is real, it is deep, and it is my favorite thing in the world.

Kelly Osbourne and Mandy Moore

Back in the early 2000s, Mandy Moore was a squeaky clean pop princess and Kelly Osbourne was a rough-around-the-edges punk rock, well, punk. But that didn't matter one bit. The two were great friends back then, and they're still friends today. They appeared together (with matching choppy haircuts) on the cover of Elle Girl back in 2003, where they were interviewed about their friendship. Honestly, I love this for both of them, and I'm so happy this is a thing.

Matthew McConaughey and Guy Fieri

How am I jealous of both of the people in this friendship? Honestly, I'm more jealous of Matthew McConaughey for getting to be friends with the host of the greatest Food Network show of all time, Diners, Drive-Ins, and Dives. Guy Fieri is a legend, and yes, I do have a t-shirt with his face on it. This is a somewhat unexpected yet delightful bromance that I'm happy to report is completely real. They've been friends for over 15 years, and Matthew said some super nice things about his friend at the unveiling of Guy's star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. I just love everything about this.

Pitbull and John Travolta

Apparently, we have Pitbull to thank for John Travolta's decision to go bald. Not a joke. They're such good friends that John calls Pitbull "Armando," which is his real name, I guess. Their friendship may be just a couple of years old, but we have a feeling this is a lifetime connection in the making.

Betty White and Jennifer Love Hewitt

Betty White and Jennifer Love Hewitt hit it off when they worked on a Hallmark movie together in 2011, and the rest is history. Now they hang out drinking cocktails and eating gummy bears. If that's not an ideal friendship situation, I don't know what is. Are they by any chance looking for a third member of their group? Perhaps a wise-cracking internet writer? Just saying.

Meryl Streep and 50 Cent

50 Cent has been spotted hanging with Meryl Streep on the sidelines at basketball games, and this has rubbed some people the wrong way, considering the rapper's history with domestic violence accusations and Meryl Streep's apparent commitment to women's rights. So it is a little strange that they'd be hanging out together. That makes this the definition of an unlikely celebrity friendship.

Tyra Banks and Clay Aiken

Model and TV host Tyra Banks and American Idol runner-up and politician Clay Aiken are best buds. They met on her talk show in 2006, and they've kept in touch ever since. Clay told People, "I love her to death. For some reason, we just clicked when we first met, and we try to stay in touch and see each other whenever we're in town." Adorbs.

Kris Jenner and Jennifer Lawrence

Kris Jenner and Jennifer Lawrence...or should I call them Kris Jennifer...are weirdly the best of friends, despite Jennifer being closer to the age of Kris' kids. JLaw may have started out as a Kardashian-Jenner fan, but once Kris showed up to her birthday party and presented her with her cake, they became fast friends. I have a feeling these two have a lot of ridiculous fun together.

Zach Braff and Harry Styles

Zach Braff and Harry Styles have a bona fide bromance going on, and yes, it's kind of weird. Their history is a bit hazy. but it seems the two connected on Twitter — where all good relationships start — and hit it off. Eventually, Harry came to Sundance to support Zach's movie, Wish I Was Here. 

Lisa Bonet and Marisa Tomei

Lisa Bonet and Marisa Tomei aren't just friends because their names rhyme, but that's got to be part of it, right? Either way, these two are longtime best friends. Like, Marisa-Tomei-is-Zoe-Kravitz'-godmother long. You just don't think about these two running in the same Hollywood circles, but their friendship started when they worked together briefly on A Different World, and they've been besties ever since. 

Ed Sheeran and Courteney Cox

British singer-songwriter Ed Sheeran and Friends star Courteney Cox have a weirdly close relationship. In fact, Courteney let Ed live in her beach house for a few months while he was writing music. So not only are they friends. They're also roomies. Ed didn't pay rent when he was staying in the house, but he did introduce Courteney to her boyfriend, Snow Patrol frontman Johnny McDaid.

Kim Kardashian and Serena Williams

It's hard to picture the greatest athlete of all time and Kim Kardashian having a lot in common, but apparently, Kim and Serena Williams have a great friendship. They actually met because Serena used to be Kim's boss! It's safe to say they've both achieved superstar status now, so these days, they spend their time out on the town having girls' nights. 

Jessica Williams and J.K. Rowling

The story of comedian Jessica Williams getting to know Harry Potter author J.K. Rowling is a pretty great one. It's a total case of how meeting your heroes can sometimes actually work out. Jessica was a huge (I mean huge) Harry Potter fan and connected with J.K., or as her friends call her, Jojo, over Twitter. It was love at first message, and they hung out for the first time in London. The two hit it off, and when they realized they have the same birthday (July 31, also Harry Potter's bday), they spent it together. This is what you call real-life magic.

Russell Brand and Helen Mirren

This might be one of the less surprising friendships on the list just because Helen Mirren is so cool, but nevertheless, we love the connection Russell Brand and Dame Helen Mirren have. They starred together in the 2011 remake of Arthur, and they had a spark from that day forward. 

Ellen Page and Ruby Rose

For some reason, I was really surprised that not only do Ellen Page and Ruby Rose know each other, but that they're really good friends. Ruby posted such an endearing message for Ellen on her birthday a few years ago, writing, "I never knew how crucial it would be to have loyal and real friends around me in Hollywood until push came to shove and there you were every time, the most honest, the most fearless and the most loyal person I know." Well, shoot. That's really cute.

Kate Hudson and Lea Michele

Kate Hudson helped Lea Michele through one of the most difficult times in her life, when boyfriend Cory Monteith died, according to the Daily Mail. Kate opened up her home to Lea and her family when her house was completely overrun with reporters and paparazzi. "She let my family stay there, and any of my friends. She made sure that in the refrigerator were my favorite juices. I'll never really be able to thank her, truly, for what she did for me."

Rashida Jones and Natalie Portman

I would love to wear my stylish sunglasses and hang out with these two gals. Turns out Rashida Jones and Natalie Portman kind of are like a real-life Anne Perkins and Leslie Knope. They've been friends for years. These two are like two peas in a ridiculously smart, badass, good-looking pod.

Maya Rudolph and Haim

I want to hang out with these women so bad. I can't contain my excitement when I see them together. Maya Rudolph is a comedy queen, and Danielle, Este, and Alana Haim are the coolest rocker chicks out there. I don't quite know the story behind their love affair, but I feel like it probably has something to do with the fact that Maya's husband is the amazing filmmaker Paul Thomas Anderson and he has directed some of Haim's most legendary music videos.
