Ron Baker: Early Life and Family

On March 30, 1993, Ron Baker was born in Hays, Kansas. His mother’s name is Ranae Baker, and his father’s name is Neil Baker. 

Ron spent his early youth in the small rural community of Utica, Kansas, where he grew up with one sister and one brother.

He said in a 2014 interview that he has recollections of when he was very, very tiny, clutching a basketball in his dog’s doghouse when he was around 1.

His father observed that he eventually gravitated toward basketball for practical reasons. Neil said that it was easier to take Ron about.

Moreover, it was also easier to get five or six children to play basketball than playing baseball, where you’d need 12 or 14.

Ron Baker: High School Career

While Ron grew into a star at Scott Community High and aspired to play for the state’s top college program at Kansas, he was slow and steady to grow into his body.

He was only 6 feet (1.83 m) tall by the end of his junior year and had drawn interest from only two Division I programs: Arkansas–Little Rock and South Dakota State. 

Between his junior and senior seasons summertime, he grew to 6 feet 3 inches (1.91 m). 

Ron contributed to the team’s 25–1 record in his senior year. In exchange, he was selected to the Kansas 3A First-Team All-State team.

His high school basketball career peaked when he shot a game-winning put-back buzzer-beater against Minneapolis High School to win the Class 3A state title, ending with 26 points and 9 rebounds.

Consequently, Ron helped the school win its first-ever Class 3A championship, even though the school had previously made two trips.

Kansas encouraged Ron to visit the school following his senior year. Ron considered walking on, but the offer came in the middle of his high school baseball season.

Ron’s father noted that his son believed his basketball game was rusty and that he “didn’t want to humiliate himself” in a possible scrimmage.

Ron Baker: College Career

Ron chose to play for the Wichita State Shockers over Arkansas–Little Rock, Eastern Illinois, and South Dakota State.

After receiving hesitant consent from head coach Gregg Marshall, the player redshirted his first season with the Shockers.

Similarly, Baker also established himself as one of Wichita State’s top free-throw shooters in his debut season.

The player concluded the season with the most significant free-throw percentage of any qualifying player on the club (.822).

However, Ron failed to establish himself as an efficient scorer, appearing in only eighteen games due to a stress fracture in his left foot. 

Likewise, Ron established himself as one of the MVC’s top shooters heading into the 2013–14 NCAA Division I men’s basketball season. 

After concluding his first season with a double-digit scoring average, Baker was selected to the 2014 MVC All-Conference First Team and the CBE Hall of Fame Classic’s Most Valuable Player.

Ron was also a member of three Missouri Valley Conference titles (2013, 2014, 2015), nine NCAA Tournament wins, two Sweet 16 appearances (2013, 2015), and a trip to the 2013 Final Four.

The player averaged 14.0 points, 4.8 rebounds, 3.2 assists, and 1.51 steals in 31.6 minutes per game as a senior in 2015–16.

Ron Baker: Professional career

New York Knicks

Ron signed with the New York Knicks for the 2016 NBA Summer League after going undrafted in the 2016 NBA draft.

Despite a disorganized Summer League campaign, Baker signed with the Knicks on August 1, 2016, and earned an opening-night roster berth. 

He made his Knicks debut on October 25, 2016, earning five points and one rebound in seven minutes off the bench in a 117–88 defeat to the Cleveland Cavaliers.

Ron set a career-best on December 15, 2016, with 13 points in a 103–90 defeat to the Golden State Warriors.

On January 16, 2017, the player got his first career start for the Knicks, scoring 12 points in a 108–107 defeat to the Atlanta Hawks. 

Ron was sent to the Westchester Knicks, New York’s D-League/G League club during his rookie and sophomore seasons. However, Baker re-signed with the Knicks on August 7, 2017.

Ron missed the Knicks’ game against the San Antonio Spurs on January 2, 2018.

The player fractured the left orbital bone received when he was smacked in the face while blocking Anthony Davis‘ drive to the basket. 

Moreover, Ron was ruled out for the rest of the season on February 5, 2018, when it was discovered that he needed right shoulder surgery. On December 13, 2018, the Knicks released Baker.

Other Teams

Ron Baker signed a contract with the Washington Wizards on December 20, 2018. He was released on January 7, 2019, after four games.

On August 1, 2019, he signed a deal with CSKA Moscow in the VTB United League and the EuroLeague.

Similarly, Ron averaged 2.5 points per game in 27 EuroLeague appearances. He left the team on May 27, 2020.

Ron Baker: Retirement

In the May 2021 edition of Inside TBT, The Basketball Tournament’s official podcast, Ron Baker announced his retirement from basketball.

He told the presenters that he underwent hip surgery while abroad and wanted to move on with his life, hoping to enter the corporate sector.

In the 2021 TBT, he is the general manager of the AfterShocks, a TBT team mostly comprised of Wichita State basketball alumni.

The player also stated that he would not be participating in this or any future editions of TBT. 

Ron was designated project manager in the strategy and business development department of Ascension Via Christi Medical Center in Wichita, Kansas, in August 2021.

Additionally, he is an enrolled member of the Citizen Potawatomi Nation.
