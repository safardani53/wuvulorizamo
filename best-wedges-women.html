THE WEDGE sandal has long divided style and fashion lovers.

Not as elegant as a pin-thin stiletto or as slinky as the kitten heel, wedges tend to be a more practical selection, because of how much easier they are to walk in.

Whether a five inch pair, or a less towering flatform, the wedge sandal has come a long way since conception in the 1930s.

They come fash-pack approved too, with the likes of Jennifer Aniston, Amal Clooney and Gigi Hadid all being spotted in varying styles over the years.

Even Victoria Beckham swaps out her chic black pointed courts for wedges sometimes.

They work well for more laid-back, rather than fancy red carpet, occasions.

You'll often see celebs in them while out with their families, running errands around LA, or exploring while on holiday.

We think they’re on to something.

Wedges are can still elevate your outfit while feeling comfortable enough to walk around all day - not even the pros can last all day in stilettos.

But with so many styles to choose from it's not easy to pick a favourite pair, so we’ve helped by rounding up a selection of the best wedges up for grabs below.

From cork to wooden and espadrille pairs, you’re spoilt for choice.

What is the most comfortable wedge shoe?

Wedges with wide feet tend to be the most comfortable to wear.

Simply be are selling basic wedges extra wide fit for £11, reduced from £23.50.

Now that's a bargain.

Are wedges hard to walk in?

Walking in wedges is much easier because they give you a more even, stable surface to walk on.

As opposed to high heels which bring a greater risk of slippage, tripping, and general unpleasantness.

We would recommend slowly easing your way into wedges rather than braving it outdoors for the day.
