DEMI Moore paid tribute to her ex-husband Bruce Willis on Sunday with a heartfelt Father’s Day post.

Taking to Instagram, 57-year-old Demi shared a lovely family photo which included the Die Hard actor's current wife Emma and their two daughters Mabel and Evelyn.

Demi, who shares three daughters with Bruce, captioned the snap: "Happy Father’s Day, Bruce! Your kids are so lucky to have you ❤️."

Despite separating in 1998, the Hollywood stars have remained on good terms and have even been isolating together during the global coronavirus pandemic.

The pair's eldest daughter, Rumer, also took to social media to post some epic throwbacks of her and her father.

The first showed a very young-looking Bruce cradling his eldest and laying in bed with her, and were captioned: "Happy Fathers Day Papa. I love you so much."

A second post showed the father and daughter duo pulling very serious faces as Rumer jokingly wrote alongside it: "I love you Daddio. I’m so glad we aren’t dramatic people..."

And a third series of pics showed Rumer as a young child in a variety of poses with her dad, including a couple where she and her sisters Scout and Tallulah had visited their dad on film sets.

"Happy Father’s Day Daddio. Thank you for always being a weirdo and making it ok for me to be one too. I love when you make me laugh so hard my cheeks hurt," the 31-year-old wrote alongside them.

Bruce's wife Emma, 41, also marked the day with an Instagram post of eight-year-old Mabel kissing her famous dad on the nose.

Emma, who married Bruce in 2009, captioned it: "We love and adore him this much. Happy Father’s Day to one of the best."

The couple spent the day by a lake with their two daughters, which Emma documented on her Instagram Story.

The Sixth Sense actor was seen showing his girls "the art of stone skipping" and was seen seen building a fort out of large sticks.

The day before, the foursome went fishing and Emma shared a cute family pic of them all posing in the sunshine.

Their Father's Day weekend comes after the family had been self-isolating with Demi and her daughters.

A source previously told PEOPLE that the Moore-Willis clan has been "one happy family" since they began social distancing together.

"Demi and Emma are very close," the source said. "They have vacationed together many times in the past and get along great."

However, at the beginning of the coronavirus pandemic, Emma and her two daughters were at home in Los Angeles while Bruce joined Demi and their daughters at their home in Idaho.

In April, Scout revealed why Emma and her half-sisters had not been able to initially join them, revealing that one of her younger siblings had accidentally stepped on a needle while at a park in Los Angeles.
