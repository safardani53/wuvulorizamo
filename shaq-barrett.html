Shaq Barrett | Early Life, Family, And Education

Shaq Barrett was born in Baltimore, Maryland, the United States. His parents are Steven Barrett and Donyetta Barrett. 

Furthermore, the NFL player’s father is a youth football coach. Steven coached the Charm City Buccaneers. 

Hence, Shaquil was introduced to football by his father. Moreover, the father always encouraged his son to take part in physical activities and sports. 

However, there wasn’t much scope in Baltimore. On top of that, they lived on projects, so many bad influences were hovering on Shaq. 

Nevertheless, the evil influences never affected the footballer and his five siblings. They were raised well and stayed out of trouble. 

Nonetheless, Baltimore was still an unsafe place for the NFL player and his dream to grow. He was even robbed off of his wallet and cellphone. 

Although the father didn’t want to miss his son’s high school life, he sent him to a private school to ensure his proper growth. The athlete attended Boys Town in Nebraska. 

The school helped him gradually but firmly establish discipline in his life and focus on football. After completing high school, the outside linebacker chose to attend the University of Nebraska Omaha.

But the college removed its football programs after Barrett’s freshman year. As a result, the Bucs player transferred to Colorado State University to continue his college football journey.

Shaq Barrett | Football Career

High School And College Career

The athlete played high school football in a private boarding school called Boys Town. Following high school graduation, he attended the University of Nebraska Omaha to play collegiate football. 

After playing his freshman year, the college put an end to all of the football programs. As a result, Barrett moved to Colorado State University to continue his collegiate football career. 

Furthermore, the player didn’t have to sit out a year and was eligible to play right away. He had an outstanding career at CSU with the Colorado State Rams. 

Moreover, the NFL player received a lot of attention for his defensive skills. As a senior, the Bucs player was the Mountain West Conference Defensive Player of the Year title. 

NFL Career

Denver Broncos

After college graduation, the former Rams decided to enter the 2014 NFL Draft. However, he wasn’t selected by any NFL teams. 

Nevertheless, Denver Broncos and Pittsburgh Steelers wanted to sign him. Eventually, Barrett signed with the Broncos as a free agent. 

After signing, he lost tons of body fat and got into shape. Moreover, he proved to be a viable asset to the team. 

In his second season with the Broncos, he won the 2015 Super Bowl against the Carolina Panthers. Moreover, he was allowed to play in the game that earned him his first Super Bowl ring. 

Thereafter, he stayed with the Broncos for three more seasons before signing with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers.
