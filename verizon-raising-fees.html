Verizon is raising its prices from next month with 'administrative fee' amid inflation - how it will

October 04, 2024
blog

VERIZON has announced that it will be increasing prices for customers starting next month.

As inflation climbs, prices have continued rising in nearly every sector.

The nation has seen higher gas prices, record home sales, and increasing electricity bills.

Verizon is the next large brand to announce price increases.

The company recently revealed its higher fees for its wireless customers and explained that the rise was due to inflation.

A Verizon spokesperson told CNET, "The current economic conditions impacting businesses worldwide continue to mount and despite our best efforts to mitigate the further impact, we intend to offset a portion of these costs."

For consumers, the fee will appear on customers' monthly bill as an administrative fee.

Verizon is raising the fee to $3.30 per line - a $1.35 increase.

Lines for tablets and smartwatches will also see the same increase.

However, the company said these hikes will not impact hotspots.

For business accounts, the fee will show up as an "economic adjustment charge."

The charge will be $2.20 per line.

The company also reported that tablets and non-smartphones will be charged 98 cents per month.

Those with multiple lines will see the biggest price difference since the fees will be charged per line.

The fees will go into effect June 16.

How to lower the cost of your bill

Even though the fee increases are unavoidable, there are still ways to bring your costs down.

Check your plan

Comparing other plans can be a great way to see what companies offer and if its other options better meet your needs.

Share a plan

If the new price increases will impact your wallet, try sharing a plan with a trusted family member or friend.

Many times, solo plans are more costly than sharing an account, so this could be the best option.

Switch providers

This can be a great alternative for those looking to make a change.

You might find that another company has much better rates, saving you cash each month.

Some companies will even offer you a cheaper rate or deals upon signing.

These options, in addition to other small changes, can make your bills go down and maybe offer some much-needed financial relief.
