Eventually, after bouncing from one club to another, Silva now plays for RB Leipzig. His paycheck is reported to be around €23 million.

11. Ciro Immobile

As an Italian professional soccer player, Ciro Immobile is not immobile when it comes to being one of the best soccer strikers. When talking about his team, he plays for Serie A club Lazio and the Italy national team.

Beginning his career at Sorrento, Immobile was considered one of the team’s best. So it wasn’t surprising to see top teams wanted him; finally, in 2009, Juventus purchased the Italian player.

His popularity and demand were soo high that he was loaned to multiple Series B clubs. The most famous one was his time with Pescara. During his time in the leagues with Pescara, Immobile won the league title as the top scorer.

10. Patrik Schick

Our 10th-ranking striker is, if not the best, then surely one of the best soccer strikers from the Czech Republic. To clarify, Patrik Schick is a Czech professional soccer player who plays as a striker for Bayer Leverkusen and the Czech Republic national team.

Starting his career with a local club Sparta Prague, Schick proved himself worthy by rising through the ranks, becoming the best soccer striker in his city. 

He joined the Bohemians in 2016 and moved to Roma, Italy. After being with the Bohemians for almost 4 years, Schick moved to Germany and joined RB Leipzig on a season-long loan before finally moving to Bayer Leverkusen.

Regarding his national plays, Schick has made over 30 caps for the national team and helped them reach the quarter-finals of the European Championship in 2020. He won the Goal of the Tournament and the Silver Boot in this tournament.

9. Kylian Mbappe

Born on 20th December 1998, Kylian Mbappe is a French professional soccer player who plays as a striker and a forward for Paris Saint-Germain and the France national team.

However, Mbappe is not just the best soccer striker but also one of the best players in the world. Most notably, he is known for his agility and dribbling skills.

Mbappe started his professional career with Monaco, a Ligue 1 club, in 2015 when he was 16. During his time with Monaco, he won the title of “Ligue 1 Young Player of the Year” and the Golden Boy award.

Finally, he signed for Paris-Germain in 2017 on an initial loan, later made permanent in 2018. This transfer was worth more than €180 million. 

Following this, Mbappe also became the youngest French player to score a goal at a world Cup, not to mention: that he is the second teenager, after Pelé, to score a goal in a world Cup Final.

In 2022, sources informed ESPN that Kylian Mbappe was pushing to leave Paris Saint-Germain in January due to the breakdown in his relationship with the team.

The news comes just five months after Mbappe signed a new contract with PSG. According to sources, he doesn’t like the way the club is going on and off the field.

4. Karim Benzema

Born on 19th December 1987, Karim Benzema is a professional soccer player who plays as a striker for Real Madrid and the France national team. Widely known for his assists and goal scores, Benzema is regarded as one of the best soccer strikers of his generation.

Beginning his professional career with Olympique Lyonnais in 2005, Benzema contributed to three Ligue 1 title wins.

Benzema was named the National Union of Footballers Professional (UNFP) Player of the Year and in the Team of the Year, finishing as the League’s top goalscorer and winning his fourth title and first Coupe de France in 2008.

Benzema’s fame and glory reached higher after joining Real Madrid for €35 million ($36.5 m).

The French national team announced on Saturday that Karim Benzema would not participate in the 2022 World Cup because of a leg injury.

The star striker had pain in his upper leg and had to leave a practice session early. He injured his femoral rectus, a quadriceps muscle, according to an MRI.
