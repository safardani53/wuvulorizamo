BEAU Dermott wowed the judges when she auditioned for Britain's Got Talent back in 2016.

She was just 13 at the time - and Amanda Holden was so taken by Beau's voice that she used her Golden Buzzer vote, sending her straight to the final.

After Beau's performance of Defying Gravity, Amanda pressed the buzzer and later gushed: "You were so, so amazing!"

Simon Cowell said: "We weren't expecting that." And Alesha Dixon added: "That was fantastic."

Beau finished in fifth place in the competition - with magician Richard Jones eventually winning - but that didn't stop her.

Shortly after her time on the show Beau signed a record deal with Decca, who insured her voice for £1m.

Tom Lewis, director of A&R, said at the time: "Beau’s voice is one in a million – and that’s why it needs insuring!

“Having such an incredible talent at such a young age is truly remarkable, and we’re looking forward to working with Beau as she develops into a young star."

She released her debut album Brave a year later, which was a compilation of covers of songs from musicals.

Beau is still singing and earlier this year promised her social media followers that she's got "lots of exciting" projects coming up.

She recently celebrated her 18th birthday and shared a string of snaps from her party on Instagram.
